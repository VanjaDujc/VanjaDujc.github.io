Tips for cooking

  1. May  I cook on olive oil or is it better to use it crude?

    2. Olive oil is a natural source of vitamins A, D, E and K and bio phenols that prevent oxidation processes to take place inside human body. None of this substances is resistible to heat, thus by heating oil they disintegrate. The same goes for aromatic compounds that give the olive oil typical flavour and aroma. In order that the oil should maintain the important substances as well as its typical flavour, we recommend that you stain, bake or roast the food in as little amount of olive oil as possible and add the major quantity of olive oil once the dish is already prepared. Thus the majority of olive oil will preserve all its ‘beneficial’ substances and the dish will have a rich and full flavour.



  2. May I fry on olive oil?

    3. Contradictory to common opinion that it is harmful to fry on olive oil, using olive oil for frying is less harmful than using the majority of other oils. The burning temperature of olive oil is at around 210oC, meaning that only at that temperature the fatty acids begin to disintegrate and toxic compound begin to develop. Compared to sunflower oil or butter, which temperature of burning is at 180oC, that is, the temperature on which we normally fry, the burning temperature of olive oil is quite high. But you should not forget that whenever you heat olive oil the favourable substances, vitamins and antioxidants as well as the flavour is lost.



  3. Olive oil is tastes so different; how should I know which olive oil should I combine with which kind of food?
    Generally speaking we use mild and sweet olive oils with dishes that have gentle taste, while we use more bitter, pungent or robust olive oils with dishes that have more intensive taste. Usually we combine milder olive oils with cold dishes (carpaccio, cold starters, salads, cheese, fruit), while intensive olive oil is an ideal combination for hot dishes (minestrone, pasta, vegetable soup). We use mild olive oils so that we drip them over the dish that is already prepared, while it is not bad to pour some intensive olive oil just into the sauce or soup while it is still cooking. If you use a gentle olive with a heavy, strong dish, the characteristic flavour of the dish will cover the flavour of the oil. Similarly, if you use some intensive, bitter olive oil with a gentle dish, the flavour of the oil will cover that of the dish.  Anyhow, keep experimenting!
