What they say

L'extravergine: guida ai migliori oli del mondo di qualità accertata 2007
Extra virgin olive oil, monocultivar maurino

Already the third time in our guide and always with excellent products: congratulations to Vanja Dujc, owner and founder of the family farm which he himself created about twenty years ago and is now extending on about 3,5 ha of olive groves. There are about 1050 trees planted, some of them local cultivars, typical of that area, as Istrian White or Buga, some of them imported from Italy as Maurino. This year about 14 tons of olive fruit were produced, what gave about 2200 l of extra virgin olive oil. Besides Extra virgin olive oil ‘Vanja' with lable of Protected Denomination of Origin, our panel wants to draw your attention on the delicious monocultivar Maurino. It presents to our eye with a limpid, nice, intensively golden yellow colour. Its fragrance is intensive and rich, with sensations of artichoke, wild chicory, radicchio and fresh aromatic sensations of mint, rosemary and basil. Its taste is intensive and complex, rich with nuances of wildflowers, fresh nuts and almonds. The sensations of spicy and bitter are equilibrated. It is delicious on salads with boletus, marinated swordfish, minestrones with beans, vegetable antipastos, fish dishes, pork meat and seasoned cheese.


Der Feinschmecker, das intenationale Gourment-Journal; Bookazine Nr. 4, Alles über Olivenöl; Jahreszeiten Verlag Gmbh, Hamburg 2006
Vanja Dujc- extra virgin olive oil (blend)

We congratulate Vanja Dujc, the best olive oil producer from Slovenia! Likewise the previous year 2005, this year Dujc proved to be one of the world's best olive oil producers. Dujc produces his oil in Koper, in the area Slovene Istria, on 4 ha of olive groves, where, besides the olive cultivars, typical of this region, some Italian cultivars (Leccino, Frantoio) also grow. The blend has a charming fresh aroma of apples, mint and oregano. On the taste the nuances of artichoke, green tomato and green banana prevail. The oil distinguishes itself by a pleasant sensation of bitter. It concludes with nice spicy sensations. We recommend it for grilled boletus or mussel soup.

Vanja Dujc - Maurino

Monocultivar maurino has the same intensive aroma as Vanja's blend, but it is a bit sweeter and extremely harmonic. This oil goes well with pasta dishes, grilled salmon and lobsters.

Extra virgin olive oil Vanja

Likewise a year ago, we would like to draw your attention on the excellent extra virgin olive oil Vanja, which presents to our eye with a limpid, golden yellow colour accompanied by firm green casts. It distinguishes itself by a rich, intensive and complex fragrance, in which elegant vegetal sensations of artichoke, thistle and fresh aromatic nuances of parsley and basilica prevail. Its taste is fine, intensive and rich, and reveals sensations of freshly mown grass, lettuce, chicory and ripe almonds. The sensations of bitter and spicy outstand. This oil is excellent on salads with champignons, white fishes in oven, antipastos with mussels, marinated tuna fish, vegetable soups with grains, grilled white meat and seasoned cheese.

Extra virgin olive oil Vanja

The extra virgin olive oil Vanja presents to our eye with a limpid, intensive, nice golden yellow colour with soft casts of green. Its aroma is complex and very harmonic, fine and peculiar, marked by sensations of citrus fruits, ripe banana and elegant sensations of black pepper and vanilla. In the mouth it feels rich and noble, endowed with vegetable sensations of fresh mown grass and aromatic nuances of rosemary and salvia. The sensations of bitter and spicy outstand, but are in harmony. It is delicious on antipastos with legumes, mixed salads, marinated cod fish, minestrones, fish soups, antipastos with mussels, grilled white fish, grilled pork meat and on fresh cheese.


Der Feinschmecker, das intenationale Gourment-Journal; Sonderheft Nr. 8, Gesund genießen; Jahreszeiten Verlag Gmbh, Hamburg 2005.
Istra, Vanja Dujc

A producer from Slovenia decided to participate on our assessment for the first time – and he surprised us greatly with his aromatic, harmonic extra virgin olive oil. Vanja Dujc from Slovene Istra gained his experience from various Italian olive oil producers, but his olive oil from almost unknown cultivars as Itrana, Piranska buga or Istrian White received many international recognitions. Sensations of pear, parsley, tarragon and almond are as much enchanting as a spicy sensation of pepper that shows at the end. It is most delicious with bread and cheese.
